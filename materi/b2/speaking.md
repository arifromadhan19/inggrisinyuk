## Day 1 — Presenting a Clear Argument (claim + support)

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Present a structured argument — state a clear claim, then support it with credible evidence and reasoning, following the FCE/B2 "develop a clear argument" descriptor.

**Key expressions:**
- Stating a claim: *My position is that... / I firmly believe that... / I'd argue that...*
- Supporting evidence: *This is supported by... / A clear example of this is... / Research shows that...*
- Strengthening the argument: *What's more... / This is further reinforced by... / Crucially,...*
- Acknowledging limits: *That said... / Of course, this isn't the whole picture, but...*

**B2 sentence patterns:**
- *My claim is [position]. This is supported by [evidence 1] and further reinforced by [evidence 2].*
- *I'd argue that [claim], primarily because [reason]. A clear example of this is [specific case].*
- *While there are exceptions, the evidence strongly suggests that [claim].*

**Pronunciation focus (suprasegmental, B2):**
- Sentence stress on the claim itself — the main verb/noun in the claim should carry primary stress
- Rhythm: argumentative speech tends to have a steady, deliberate rhythm — avoid rushing through evidence
- Word stress: *argument* /ˈɑːɡjʊmənt/, *evidence* /ˈevɪdəns/ — both stress the first syllable

## Day 2 — Defending Your Opinion Against Counter-Arguments

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Sustain a position when challenged — restate your stance, acknowledge the counter-argument, and explain why your view still holds. This is the B2 "sustain views" descriptor in action.

**Key expressions:**
- Acknowledging the challenge: *That's a fair point, but... / I see where you're coming from, however... / I understand the concern, but...*
- Holding your position: *I still maintain that... / My view hasn't changed because... / Even so, I'd argue...*
- Reinforcing with new reasoning: *What you're missing is... / The key issue is still... / Let me clarify why...*
- Conceding partially without losing ground: *You're right that [minor point], but the main issue is [your core point].*

**B2 sentence patterns:**
- *That's a fair point, but I still believe [position] because [reason], which outweighs [counter-argument].*
- *I understand the concern about [counter], however [rebuttal] makes my original point even stronger.*
- *You raise a valid issue, but it doesn't really undermine my main argument that [position].*

**Pronunciation focus (suprasegmental, B2):**
- Sentence stress on contrast words when defending: *STILL believe, EVEN so, the KEY issue*
- Steady, unhurried rhythm when conceding a point — rushing can sound defensive
- Word stress: *maintain* /meɪnˈteɪn/ — stress second syllable; *undermine* /ˌʌndəˈmaɪn/ — stress third syllable

## Day 3 — Discussing Pros and Cons in Depth

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Go beyond surface-level advantages/disadvantages (B1 Day 10) into deep elaboration — explaining *why* each point matters, weighing significance, and reaching a weighted conclusion.

**Key expressions:**
- Deep elaboration: *What makes this significant is... / The real benefit lies in... / This matters because, in practice,...*
- Weighing importance: *This carries more weight than... / Although both are valid, [X] is arguably more pressing because...*
- Nuanced conclusion: *On balance, weighing both sides carefully... / If I had to prioritise, I'd say...*

**B2 sentence patterns:**
- *One major advantage is [X], which matters because [deeper reason]. However, this is offset by [disadvantage], particularly when [condition].*
- *Weighing both sides, I'd say [side] carries more weight, mainly because [reasoning that goes beyond the surface].*
- *Although [advantage] sounds appealing, the real cost is [disadvantage], which can outweigh it in practice.*

**Pronunciation focus (suprasegmental, B2):**
- Sentence stress on weighing language: *carries MORE weight, REALLY matters, in PRACTICE*
- Rhythm: deliberate pacing when transitioning between pro and con (a brief pause signals the shift)
- Word stress: *significant* /sɪɡˈnɪfɪkənt/, *outweigh* /aʊtˈweɪ/ — stress second syllable

## Day 4 — Hypothesising and Speculating (What if...?)

- Hypothesising: *If [hypothetical condition], [speculated result]... / Suppose that... / Imagine if...*
- Degrees of certainty: *It's likely that... / I'd guess that... / There's a good chance that... / It's hard to say, but possibly...*
- Exploring implications: *This would probably lead to... / One likely consequence would be... / It might also mean that...*

**B2 sentence patterns:**
- *If [hypothetical event] had/were [condition], I think [speculated result] would probably happen, mainly because [reasoning].*
- *Suppose [scenario] — it's likely that [consequence], although there's also a chance that [alternative outcome].*
- *Imagine if [condition]. This would probably lead to [result], which in turn might cause [secondary effect].*

**Pronunciation focus (suprasegmental, B2):**
- Sentence stress on certainty markers: *LIKELY, PRObably, MIGHT, there's a good CHANCE*

- Word stress: *hypothetical* /ˌhaɪpəˈθetɪkəl/ — stress third syllable; *consequence* /ˈkɒnsɪkwəns/ — stress first syllable

## Day 6 — Giving a Structured Mini-Presentation

- *Today, I'd like to talk about [topic]. I'll cover two main points: first [point 1], and then [point 2].*

## Day 7 — Handling Q&A After a Presentation

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Respond to spontaneous questions coherently after presenting — the B2 "interaction" descriptor, requiring quick, organised thinking under mild pressure.

**Key expressions:**
- Buying thinking time: *That's a good question. Let me think about that for a moment... / Good point — here's how I'd address that...*
- Structuring a spontaneous answer: *There are a couple of reasons for that... / The short answer is... but let me elaborate...*
- Handling a question you can't fully answer: *I don't have all the details, but my understanding is... / That's a fair challenge — I'd need to look into that further, but...*

**B2 sentence patterns:**
- *That's a good question. The short answer is [brief answer], but let me elaborate: [elaboration].*
