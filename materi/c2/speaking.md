## Day 1 — Conveying Finer Shades of Meaning Precisely

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Craft goal:** Choose between closely related words to convey an exact shade of meaning — the CEFR C2 "differentiating finer shades of meaning" descriptor, the defining mastery skill that anchors this entire level.

**Key expressions:**
- Precision framing: *The word that actually fits here is..., not... / There's a real difference between [word A] and [word B], even though they're often used interchangeably.*
- Justifying the precise choice: *I chose [word] specifically because it carries [exact connotation] that [near-synonym] doesn't.*
- Native-like self-correction for precision: *Actually, "concerned" isn't quite right — "uneasy" captures it better.*

**C2 sentence patterns:**
- *The word that actually fits here is [precise word], not [near-synonym] — they're often used interchangeably, but there's a real difference.*
- *I chose [word] specifically because it carries [exact connotation] that [near-synonym] simply doesn't capture.*
- *Actually, on reflection, [word A] isn't quite right — [word B] captures it more precisely.*

**Pronunciation focus (native prosody, C2):**
- Subtle stress shift signalling a precision correction mid-sentence — a brief, natural beat before the more exact word
- Near-native rhythm throughout; this Day is about lexical precision, not pronunciation correction
- This is genuinely about word choice — pronunciation notes here will likely be minimal to none for most speakers at this level

---

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)

1. **Describe a moment where the precise word made all the difference — replace a vague description with one that captures the finer shade of meaning exactly.**

2. **Distinguish between two near-synonymous words by explaining exactly which finer shade each one captures.**

3. **Describe a feeling that English doesn't have one perfect word for, choosing the combination of words that comes closest.**

## Day 2 — Register-Shifting Mid-Conversation (formal to casual)

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Craft goal:** Deliberately shift register mid-conversation in response to the audience or mood — the C2 "register-shifting" craft skill, a controlled performance ability rather than a new grammatical function.

**Key expressions:**
- Formal register markers: *I'd like to outline... / With regard to... / It would be advisable to...*
- Casual register markers: *So basically... / Honestly, it's kind of like... / Yeah, no worries, it'll work out.*
- Signalling the shift naturally: *Anyway — switching gears a bit — ... / Off the record, though...*

**C2 sentence patterns:**
- *I'd like to outline the key considerations here... [shift] ...but honestly? It's kind of a no-brainer once you look at it.*
- *With regard to the proposal, the recommendation is to proceed... [shift] ...look, basically, it's a good call.*
- *Off the record, though, between us — [casual aside] — that's really what's going on.*

**Pronunciation focus (native prosody, C2):**
- Audible shift in pace and pitch range accompanying the register change — formal register tends toward more even pitch, casual toward wider, more relaxed variation
- The shift itself should sound natural and unforced, not like flipping a switch
- Contractions and reduced forms ("kind of," "gonna") appear naturally only in the casual register portion

## Day 4 — Handling Humour, Irony and Sarcasm

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Craft goal:** Produce and recognise irony, dry humour, and gentle sarcasm appropriately — the C2 pragmatic mastery descriptor, requiring tonal control that signals "this isn't literal" without ever stating it.

**Key expressions:**
- Dry humour markers: *Oh, brilliant. / Well, that went well, didn't it. / Sure, because that's exactly what I needed today.*
- Gentle sarcasm: *Wonderful — another meeting. Just what I was hoping for. / Great, more paperwork. My favourite.*
- Verbal irony framing: *Funny how that worked out... / Of all the days for that to happen...*

**C2 sentence patterns:**
- *Oh, brilliant — exactly what I needed today, another deadline moved up.*
- *Sure, because nothing says "relaxing weekend" quite like a surprise audit.*
- *Funny how that worked out — the one day I left my umbrella at home.*

**Pronunciation focus (native prosody, C2):**
- Flat or slightly exaggerated intonation on ironic statements — the mismatch between tone and literal content is what signals irony, not the words themselves
- A brief, knowing pause before or after the ironic line can heighten the effect without overdoing it
- Dry delivery (understated, not exaggerated) for true "dry humour," distinct from broader, more obvious sarcasm

## Day 5 — Persuading at a Sophisticated Level (subtle rhetoric)

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Craft goal:** Persuade without the listener feeling pushed — C2 advanced suasion, where the persuasive structure is so smooth it feels like the listener arrived at the conclusion themselves, distinct from C1's more visible rhetorical devices.

**Key expressions:**
- Invisible framing: *Have you ever noticed how... / It's interesting that, once you think about it,...*
- Leading questions that do the work: *What would happen if...? / Doesn't it make you wonder why...?*
- Soft conclusion-planting: *Which kind of makes you think, doesn't it...*

**C2 sentence patterns:**
- *Have you ever noticed how [observation]? It's interesting, once you think about it, because [implication].*
- *What would happen if [scenario]? ...Which kind of makes you wonder, doesn't it.*
- *It's funny — the more you look at [topic], the more it seems like [conclusion], though I'll let you draw your own conclusions.*

**Pronunciation focus (native prosody, C2):**
- Unhurried, conversational rhythm throughout — any urgency in delivery undermines the "this is just a natural thought" effect
- Genuine-sounding rising intonation on leading questions, as if truly curious rather than rhetorically loaded
- A light, almost throwaway tone on the conclusion itself, letting it land softly rather than being delivered as a hard closing argument

## Day 6 — Diplomatic Communication in Tense Situations

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Craft goal:** Maintain total register control while genuinely defusing tension — C2 diplomacy under real conflict, more demanding than C1's strong-but-diplomatic disagreement (which assumed composure was already available).

**Key expressions:**
- Defusing without dismissing: *I can see this matters a lot to both of you... / Let's take a breath and look at what's actually at stake here.*
- Delivering unwelcome news gently: *I want to be straightforward with you, and I also want to do this with care... / This isn't the news either of us wanted, but...*
- De-escalating without conceding: *I hear that you're frustrated, and that's fair — and I still need to hold the line on [point].*

**C2 sentence patterns:**
- *I can see this matters a lot to both of you — let's take a breath and look at what's actually at stake here.*
- *I want to be straightforward with you, and I also want to do this with care: [unwelcome news], and here's what we can do next.*
- *I hear that you're frustrated, and that's completely fair — and I still need to hold the line on [point], for [reason].*

**Pronunciation focus (native prosody, C2):**
- Calm, low, steady pitch throughout — even tense content delivered with grounded, unhurried prosody to literally embody de-escalation
- Brief, deliberate pauses before delivering difficult content, signalling care rather than hesitation
- No defensive speed-up under pushback — pace should if anything slow slightly when tension rises
