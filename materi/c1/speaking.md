## Day 1 — Delivering a Structured Formal Debate

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Deliver a formal debate opening with a clear thesis, structured supporting points, and anticipation of the opponent's strongest objection — full CAE/C1 debating control, beyond B2's "mini debate" introduction.

**Key expressions:**
- Thesis statement: *My position is unequivocal: ... / I stand firmly behind the view that...*
- Structuring support: *This rests on three pillars: firstly... secondly... and finally...*
- Anticipating objections: *I anticipate the objection that..., but this overlooks... / Some will argue..., yet this fails to account for...*

**C1 sentence patterns:**
- *My position is unequivocal: [thesis]. This rests on three pillars: firstly, [point 1]; secondly, [point 2]; and finally, [point 3].*
- *I anticipate the objection that [counter], but this overlooks [rebuttal point].*
- *Far from being [opponent's framing], [topic] is in fact [reframed position], for the following reasons.*

**Pronunciation focus (pragmatic intonation, C1):**
- Rhetorical stress on the thesis statement — a falling, declarative intonation signals unwavering conviction
- Contrastive stress when reframing the opponent's position: *FAR from being X, it is in fact Y*
- Word stress: *unequivocal* /ˌʌnɪˈkwɪvəkəl/ — stress fourth syllable; *thesis* /ˈθiːsɪs/ — stress first syllable

## Day 2 — Building a Persuasive Argument (rhetoric)

- Rhetorical questions: *Isn't it time we...? / What kind of society allows...? / How much longer can we...?*
- Repetition for emphasis: *This isn't just about X. It's about Y. It's about Z.*
- Tricolon (rule of three): *We need policies that are fair, sustainable, and lasting.*

**C1 sentence patterns:**
- *Isn't it time we [call to action]? This isn't just about [surface issue] — it's about [deeper value], it's about [broader stake].*

## Day 3 — Rebutting Counter-Arguments Precisely

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Rebut a counter-argument with precision — conceding only what's strictly necessary while dismantling the substance of the challenge, distinct from B2's broader "defending against counter-arguments."

**Key expressions:**
- Precise concession: *I'll grant that [narrow point], but this is a minor concession that doesn't touch the core claim.*
- Dismantling the substance: *The flaw in that argument is... / This rests on an assumption that doesn't hold, namely...*
- Reasserting with precision: *What remains true, regardless, is...*

**C1 sentence patterns:**
- *I'll grant that [narrow concession], but this doesn't touch the core claim that [position]. The flaw in the counter-argument is [specific logical or evidentiary gap].*
- *That argument rests on an assumption that doesn't hold — namely, [flawed assumption]. Once we correct for that, [position] still stands.*
- *What remains true, regardless of that objection, is [restated core claim].*

**Pronunciation focus (pragmatic intonation, C1):**
- Subtle emphasis distinguishing the conceded point (lower stress, brief) from the reasserted core claim (stronger stress, declarative)
- Contrastive stress on "doesn't touch the CORE claim" — isolating what's NOT affected by the concession
- Word stress: *assumption* /əˈsʌmpʃən/ — stress second syllable; *flaw* /flɔː/ — single syllable, careful vowel length

## Day 4 — Negotiating Complex Deals and Conditions

#### 📚 Detail Materi

**Communicative goal:** Negotiate multiple interdependent conditions simultaneously — the C1 "negotiate skillfully" descriptor, more layered than B2's single-issue compromise negotiation.

**Key expressions:**
- Bundling conditions: *Let's look at this as a package — if we adjust [condition A], that affects [condition B]...*
- Trading across conditions: *I could be flexible on [condition A] if we firm up [condition B] in return.*
- Sequencing complexity: *Let's settle [condition A] first, since it determines how we approach [condition B] and [condition C].*

**C1 sentence patterns:**
- *Let's look at this as a package: if we adjust [condition A], that has implications for [condition B], so let's address them together.*

- *Given how interconnected these terms are, I'd suggest we settle [condition A] first, as it shapes everything else.*

- Even, deliberate pacing across multiple conditions — rushing risks losing the other party in the complexity
