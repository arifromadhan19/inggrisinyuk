### DAY 1 — Introducing Yourself

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Memperkenalkan diri sendiri dalam situasi sosial dasar — menyebutkan nama, asal, pekerjaan/status, dan satu hal tentang diri sendiri. Sesuai CEFR A1 Spoken Interaction: *"can introduce him/herself and use basic greeting and leave-taking expressions."*
Ekspresi kunci yang wajib disebut Kak Ara: (1) "My name is... / I'm [name]" — dua varian yang sama-sama natural, (2) "I'm from... / I live in..." — perbedaan nuansanya (asal vs tempat tinggal sekarang), (3) "I'm a [noun profesi]" — jangan lupa "a/an" sebelum pekerjaan, (4) "I enjoy / I like + verb-ing" — pola dasar menyebut aktivitas favorit.
Fokus A1: kalimat pendek dan sederhana, satu informasi per kalimat. Tidak perlu elaborate. Kesalahan yang paling wajar dan perlu diantisipasi: missing verb to be ("I from Jakarta" → "I'm from Jakarta"), lupa artikel "a" sebelum profesi ("I'm student" → "I'm **a** student").

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **What is your name and where are you from?** / *Siapa namamu dan dari mana asalmu?*
2. **What do you do - are you a student or do you work?** / *Kamu ngapain sekarang - masih sekolah/kuliah atau sudah bekerja?*
3. **Tell me one thing you enjoy doing in your free time.** / *Ceritakan satu hal yang kamu suka lakukan di waktu luang.*

Contoh referensi jawaban (AI generate versi sendiri saat runtime, bukan di-embed ke prompt): *"My name is Rina. I'm from Surabaya."* / *"I'm a university student."* / *"I enjoy watching films in my free time."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum A1 Day 1: *"My name Rina"* → **"My name is Rina"** (missing verb to be); *"I from Jakarta"* → **"I'm from Jakarta"** (missing am); *"I student"* → **"I'm a student"** (missing am + artikel). Kalau sudah benar: kolom Native Speaker tandai OK.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — segmental focus: "name" /neɪm/ (bukan */name/* — vokal /eɪ/ diftong, bukan /a/ pendek Bahasa Indonesia); "from" /frɒm/ (konsonan cluster /fr/ awal — Bahasa Indonesia tidak punya pola ini); "enjoy" /ɪnˈdʒɔɪ/ (konsonan /dʒ/ seperti huruf 'j' dalam bahasa Inggris, bukan /y/ Bahasa Indonesia). Tidak ada isu pelafalan: tulis "-".
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran & Naturalness** — di A1 sangat wajar kalau jawaban sangat singkat (1–2 kalimat). Cek: apakah user berani membuat kalimat penuh (subjek + verb) vs hanya menyebutkan kata lepas. Kalau sudah ada kalimat lengkap: positif, encourage.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I enjoy..." atau "I like..." sama-sama natural; hindari "I very like" (Bahasa Indonesia transfer); "I'm from..." lebih alami dari "I come from..." untuk perkenalan singkat A1.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **Analisa Level CEFR** — tips naik ke A2: coba tambah satu detail ("I'm from Bandung, **in West Java**"), gunakan "and" untuk menggabungkan dua informasi dalam satu kalimat.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi Sesi** — lancar di Q1–Q2: tambahkan "Can you tell me one more thing about yourself?" sebelum Q3. Macet: Pattern Drill → *"My name is [___]. I'm from [___]. I'm a [___]."* — ajak user isi langsung, lalu ulangi Q yang sama.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas koreksi Day 1 (urutan kepentingan): (1) missing verb to be — sangat umum dari pengaruh Bahasa Indonesia yang tidak membutuhkan "adalah" dalam pola ini; (2) lupa "a/an" sebelum noun profesi. Jangan overload — ini sesi pertama, dorong **keberanian ngomong** lebih dari akurasi. Kalau user sangat singkat ("Arif. Jakarta."), ajak expand dengan santai: "Bagus berani ngomong! Coba jadikan kalimat penuh ya — 'My name is...'" Jangan sebut kata "salah" di sesi pertama kalau bisa dihindari — framing koreksi sebagai "versi yang lebih natural."

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab Q1 "My name Arif, I from Jakarta" → Kak Ara buat tabel: "My name Arif" → "My name is Arif", "I from Jakarta" → "I'm from Jakarta". Catatan Pelafalan: cek "name" /neɪm/. Analisa Kelancaran: singkat tapi ada upaya kalimat — positif untuk A1 pertama. Saran Kata: "I'm from" lebih natural. CEFR tip: tambahkan kota provinsi. Adaptasi: lancar di Q1 → lanjut Q2 langsung. Kalau user menjawab Q3 hanya dengan satu kata (misalnya "film"), ajak jadikan kalimat: "Great! Coba jadikan kalimat: 'I enjoy watching films.'"

### DAY 2 — Asking Questions

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Membentuk dan mengajukan pertanyaan dasar dalam bahasa Inggris — menggunakan kata tanya (what, where, when, how), pertanyaan yes/no dengan "do/does", dan pola pertanyaan frekuensi "how often." Sesuai CEFR A1 Spoken Interaction: *"can ask and answer simple questions about personal details."*
Ekspresi kunci yang wajib disebut Kak Ara: (1) Kata tanya dasar — "What...?", "Where...?", "When...?", "How...?", "Who...?" dan cara menempatkannya di awal kalimat; (2) Pola "Do you + verb...?" untuk pertanyaan yes/no tentang kebiasaan/suka-tidak suka; (3) "How often do you...?" untuk frekuensi; (4) Intonasi naik (rising intonation) di akhir pertanyaan yes/no — salah satu fitur prosodi paling mendasar yang perlu disebut.
Fokus A1: kesalahan paling umum adalah urutan kata yang salah (word order) karena pengaruh Bahasa Indonesia. "You like what?" bukan pola Inggris — harus "What do you like?" Ini poin kritis hari ini.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Ask me two questions about my daily life - use 'What' or 'Where'.** / *Tanya aku dua pertanyaan tentang kehidupan sehari-hariku - pakai 'What' atau 'Where'.*
2. **Now ask me two questions about my hobby - use 'Do you...?' or 'How often...?'** / *Sekarang tanya aku dua pertanyaan tentang hobiku - pakai 'Do you...?' atau 'How often...?'*
3. **You just met someone new. Ask them three questions to get to know them.** / *Kamu baru ketemu seseorang. Tanya mereka tiga pertanyaan untuk saling mengenal.*

Contoh referensi jawaban Q1: *"What time do you wake up? Where do you work?"* | Q2: *"Do you like sports? How often do you exercise?"* | Q3: *"What is your name? Where are you from? What do you do?"*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum Day 2: *"You like what?"* → **"What do you like?"** (inverted word order); *"Where you work?"* → **"Where do you work?"** (missing auxiliary "do"); *"What is your hobby?"* — sudah benar (to be question, tidak butuh "do"). Cek setiap pertanyaan yang user buat.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "where" /weə/ (diftong /eə/, bukan /wer/ satu bunyi); "what" /wɒt/ (vokal /ɒ/ pendek, bukan /wat/); "often" /ˈɒf.ən/ (huruf "t" tidak dibaca dalam pengucapan natural UK). Tidak ada isu: tulis "-".
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran & Naturalness** — cek apakah pertanyaan user grammatically inverted (tanda bertanya intonasi naik di akhir). Kaku: pertanyaan langsung tanpa inversion. Natural: sudah ada "do/does" di posisi benar.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "What do you do for fun?" lebih natural dari "What is your hobby?" untuk konteks percakapan santai; "How long have you...?" (untuk durasi) adalah upgrade A2 yang bisa diperkenalkan kalau user sudah lancar.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **Analisa Level CEFR** — tips naik ke A2: mulai pakai follow-up question — setelah dapat jawaban, tanya lagi berdasarkan jawabannya ("Oh you like football — which team do you support?").
8️⃣ **Adaptasi Sesi** — lancar: minta buat pertanyaan dengan "Why...?" atau "How many...?" (lebih menantang). Macet di word order: Pattern Drill → "[Kata tanya] + do + you + [verb]?" — contoh: "What do you eat?", "Where do you go?", "When do you sleep?" lalu ulangi Q yang sama.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas koreksi Day 2: (1) word order (bukan kata-katanya yang salah, tapi urutan — "You eat what?" vs "What do you eat?"); (2) missing auxiliary "do" di pertanyaan present tense. Poin pedagogis penting: di Bahasa Indonesia urutan kata pertanyaan = urutan kalimat biasa (cukup ganti intonasi), sedangkan bahasa Inggris harus membalik urutan. Jelaskan ini dengan santai, bukan terlalu teknikal — "Di Inggris kita harus 'balik' kata-katanya kalau mau tanya."

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab Q1 "What is your work? Where you go every morning?" → Tabel: Q1 benar (to be question), Q2 → "Where do you go every morning?" (missing do). Pelafalan: "where" /weə/. Kelancaran: bagus ada 2 pertanyaan. Saran: "go" bisa lebih spesifik. CEFR tip: tambahkan follow-up. Adaptasi: lancar → Q2 dengan "How often...?" ; macet → drill pola "[Kata tanya] + do + you + [verb]?"

### DAY 3 — Talking About Daily Routines

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menceritakan dan mendeskripsikan rutinitas sehari-hari — apa yang dilakukan dari pagi hingga malam, kapan, dan dengan pola apa. CEFR A1: *"can describe what people do (e.g. daily activities) in very simple sentences."*
Ekspresi kunci yang wajib disebut Kak Ara: (1) "I wake up / get up at [time]..." — bangun tidur; (2) "I usually / always / sometimes + [verb]..." — adverb frekuensi sederhana; (3) "Every morning/afternoon/evening, I..." — time frame dasar; (4) "Then / After that, I..." — connector sederhana untuk urutan aktivitas; (5) "I go to [place]..." — berpindah tempat.
Fokus A1: simple present tense untuk rutinitas (bukan progressive/past). Error kritis: "I am go to work" → "I go to work" (to be tidak dipakai bersama main verb di simple present). Error lain: lupa "have" dalam "have breakfast/lunch/dinner" (lebih natural dari "eat breakfast").

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **What time do you wake up and what do you do first in the morning?** / *Jam berapa kamu bangun dan apa yang pertama kamu lakukan di pagi hari?*
2. **Describe your afternoon routine in three or four sentences.** / *Ceritakan rutinitas siang harimu dalam tiga atau empat kalimat.*
3. **What do you usually do in the evening before going to bed?** / *Apa yang biasanya kamu lakukan di malam hari sebelum tidur?*

Contoh referensi jawaban Q1: *"I wake up at six. I brush my teeth and have breakfast."* | Q2: *"After lunch, I usually rest for 30 minutes. Then I go back to work."* | Q3: *"I usually watch TV or read before I sleep."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum Day 3: *"I am wake up at 6"* → **"I wake up at 6"** (to be + main verb = salah di simple present); *"I go to work at 8 AM in the morning"* → **"I go to work at 8 AM"** (redundant — "AM" sudah menandai pagi); *"I bath"* → **"I take a shower / I have a bath"** (phrasal verb lebih natural). Sudah benar: OK.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "usually" /ˈjuːʒuəli/ (fokus: vokal pertama /juː/ panjang, dan /ʒ/ seperti "zh" yang tidak ada di Bahasa Indonesia); "morning" /ˈmɔːnɪŋ/ (vokal /ɔː/ panjang, bukan /mornin/); "routine" /ruːˈtiːn/ (stress di suku kedua).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran & Naturalness** — kaku: hanya list aktivitas tanpa connector ("I wake up. I brush teeth. I eat. I go."). Natural: ada connector "then / after that." Cek apakah ada upaya menyambung kalimat.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "have breakfast/lunch/dinner" lebih natural dari "eat breakfast" dalam percakapan santai UK/US; "commute to work" lebih natural dari "go to office" untuk rutinitas kerja harian.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **Analisa Level CEFR** — tips naik ke A2: tambahkan detail durasi atau alasan ("I usually exercise for 30 minutes **because** it makes me feel better") — connector "because" adalah jembatan ke A2.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi Sesi** — lancar: tanya "What do you do differently on weekends?" (mengintroduksi kontras kebiasaan). Macet: Pattern Drill → "Every morning I ___. Then I ___. After that I ___." — user isi 3 slot aktivitas berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara

### DAY 6 — Talking About Time

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyebutkan dan mendiskusikan waktu — jam, bagian hari, hari dalam seminggu, dan durasi. CEFR A1: *"can tell the time and talk about what time they do things."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) "It is [time]" / "It's [hour] o'clock / half past / quarter to/past"; (2) preposisi waktu — "at [clock time]", "in the morning/afternoon/evening", "on [day]", "for [duration]"; (3) "It takes [duration] to [verb]" — untuk durasi perjalanan/aktivitas.

3. **How do you spend your time on weekends? Tell me about a typical Saturday or Sunday.** / *Bagaimana kamu menghabiskan waktu di akhir pekan? Ceritakan tentang Sabtu atau Minggu biasamu.*

Contoh referensi: *"It is seven thirty in the evening. I am studying now."* | *"I usually have lunch at twelve. Dinner is at seven."* | *"On Saturdays I sleep late. In the afternoon I go out with friends."*

### DAY 7 — Likes and Dislikes

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Mengungkapkan kesukaan dan ketidaksukaan tentang hal-hal sehari-hari — musik, makanan, aktivitas. CEFR A1: *"can describe people, places, and things using simple words and phrases, including preferences."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) "I like / I love / I enjoy [noun/verb-ing]" — preferensi positif; (2) "I don't like / I dislike / I can't stand [noun/verb-ing]" — preferensi negatif; (3) "My favorite [noun] is..." — menyebut yang paling disukai; (4) "I prefer [A] to [B]" — perbandingan sederhana.

2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "dislike" /dɪsˈlaɪk/ (stress di suku ke-2, dis-LIKE); "enjoy" /ɪnˈdʒɔɪ/ (en-JOY, bukan EN-joy); "favorite" /ˈfeɪvərɪt/ (3 suku kata: FAY-vuh-rit, bukan fa-VOR-ite).

6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan alasan yang lebih detail menggunakan "because" + kalimat lengkap + opini tambahan.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Is there anything you used to dislike but now enjoy? What changed?" (past + present). Macet: Pattern Drill → "I like ___ing because it makes me feel [adjective]." — drill 3 contoh.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) "like/love/enjoy + verb-ing" — pola paling sering salah; (2) mendorong alasan ("why") meskipun sederhana. Kalau user hanya jawab "I like music" tanpa detail, prompt mereka: "Tell me more — what kind of music?"

Pelafalan: "jazz" /dʒæz/ (vokal pendek /æ/). Kelancaran: informasi cukup untuk A1. Saran: "I'm really into jazz" lebih natural. CEFR: sudah A1+, upgrade ke A2 dengan tambah alasan.

### DAY 8 — Giving Directions

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Memberi dan meminta petunjuk arah dengan jelas dan sopan. CEFR A1: *"can ask for and give basic directions using simple language."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) instruksi arah — "Go straight", "Turn left/right", "Take the first/second road on the left/right"; (2) landmark — "It is next to / opposite / in front of / behind [place]"; (3) jarak — "Walk for [X] minutes", "It is about [X] metres away"; (4) meminta konfirmasi — "Is it far from here?"
Fokus A1: perbedaan "turn left" (imperative/instruksi) vs "on the left" (keterangan letak). Error umum: "go to left" → "turn left" atau "go left" (tanpa "to"); "opposite of" → "opposite" (tanpa "of").

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **How do you get from your home to the nearest market or store? Give simple directions.** / *Bagaimana cara pergi dari rumahmu ke pasar atau toko terdekat? Berikan petunjuk arah sederhana.*
2. **Someone asks you: "Where is the nearest ATM?" — how do you give them directions?** / *Seseorang bertanya: "Di mana ATM terdekat?" — bagaimana kamu memberi petunjuk arah?*
3. **Describe the route from your home to school, your office, or a place you go often.** / *Deskripsikan rute dari rumahmu ke sekolah, kantor, atau tempat yang sering kamu kunjungi.*

Contoh referensi: *"Go straight for two minutes. Then turn right at the traffic light. The market is on the left."* | *"Go out of this building. Turn left. The ATM is next to the minimarket."* | *"I take the bus every morning. I get off at the third stop and walk for five minutes."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "go to left" → **"turn left"**; "opposite of the bank" → **"opposite the bank"**; "in front" (tanpa noun) → **"in front of [place]"**; "go to straight" → **"go straight"**.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "straight" /streɪt/ (silent gh, sering salah jadi /stra-ait/); "opposite" /ˈɒpəzɪt/ (OP-uh-zit, bukan o-PO-sit); "traffic" /ˈtræfɪk/ (vokal pendek /æ/ di suku pertama).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah ada urutan langkah yang logis (pertama... kemudian... lalu...). Kalau tidak ada urutan, arahnya akan membingungkan.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "You can't miss it" (idiom sangat umum untuk landmark yang jelas); "It's just around the corner" (dekat sekali); "Keep walking until you see..." lebih natural dari "walk and see..."
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan estimasi waktu dan kondisi jalan — "It takes about ten minutes on foot" atau "It's a bit tricky — you need to go through the alley."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "What public transport would you recommend to get there?" (transport + arah). Macet: Pattern Drill → "Turn [left/right] at the [landmark]. It is on the [left/right]." — drill 3 rute berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) "turn left/right" bukan "go to left/right"; (2) penggunaan preposisi letak (next to, opposite, in front of, behind). Jika user memberi arah yang membingungkan, minta klarifikasi: "What landmark should I look for?" — ini juga latihan komunikasi dua arah.

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab "Go to left. Market is in front." → Tabel: "Go to left" → "Turn left" (hapus "to"); "Market is in front" → "The market is in front of [landmark]" (perlu noun setelah "in front of"). Pelafalan: "market" /ˈmɑːkɪt/. Kelancaran: terlalu singkat, perlu lebih detail. Saran: "You can't miss it" untuk closure yang natural.

### DAY 9 — Ordering Food and Drinks

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Memesan makanan dan minuman di kafe/restoran dengan sopan dan jelas. CEFR A1: *"can ask for things and make simple transactions using basic polite expressions."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) memesan — "I would like / I'll have / Can I get [item], please?"; (2) meminta menu/bill — "Could I see the menu?", "Can I have the bill, please?", "Check, please."; (3) menyatakan preferensi — "without [ingredient]", "Is there [item] in this?", "I'm allergic to [food]"; (4) respons — "Of course!", "Certainly!", "Right away!".
Fokus A1: "I would like" (formal, polite) lebih baik dari "I want" untuk konteks restoran. Error umum: "I want [item]" tanpa "please" — gramatikalnya benar tapi kurang sopan; "Give me" terlalu direct/kasar dalam konteks restoran Inggris.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **You are at a cafe. What would you like to order? Tell the waiter your order in English.** / *Kamu sedang di kafe. Apa yang ingin kamu pesan? Sampaikan pesananmu ke pelayan dalam bahasa Inggris.*
2. **How do you ask a waiter for the menu, and how do you ask for the bill at the end?** / *Bagaimana kamu meminta menu kepada pelayan, dan bagaimana kamu meminta tagihan di akhir makan?*
3. **Describe your favorite Indonesian dish and how you would order it at a restaurant.** / *Deskripsikan makanan Indonesia favoritmu dan bagaimana kamu akan memesannya di restoran.*

Contoh referensi: *"I would like a cup of coffee and a slice of chocolate cake, please."* | *"Excuse me, could I see the menu? ... Can I have the bill, please?"* | *"I'd like to order nasi goreng. Can I have it without the egg, please? I am allergic to eggs."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah

2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "menu" /ˈmenjuː/ (MEN-you, bukan ME-nu); "receipt" /rɪˈsiːt/ (re-SEET, huruf p tidak dibaca); "allergic" /əˈlɜːdʒɪk/ (ah-LER-jik).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah user menggunakan "please" dan ekspresi sopan. Untuk konteks layanan, kesopanan adalah bagian dari komunikasi yang efektif.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I'll go with..." natural dan casual untuk memesan; "Could you make that..." untuk modifikasi pesanan; "What do you recommend?" untuk bertanya rekomendasi kepada pelayan.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: belajar cara mengeluh dengan sopan jika ada yang salah — "Excuse me, I think there's been a mistake with my order."

### DAY 10 — Making Small Talk

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Memulai dan menjaga percakapan ringan dengan orang yang baru dikenal atau kenalan. CEFR A1: *"can make and respond to simple suggestions and invitations, using basic polite expressions."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) pembuka — "How are you?", "Nice weather today, isn't it?", "Are you from around here?"; (2) respons — "Not bad, thanks. How about you?", "Yes, quite nice!"; (3) pengisi jeda — "Actually...", "By the way...", "You know what..."; (4) mengakhiri — "Anyway, it was nice talking to you!", "See you around!".
Fokus A1: small talk bukan hanya pertanyaan — juga reaksi dan "active listening cues" seperti "Oh really?", "That's interesting!", "I see.". Error umum: berhenti setelah menjawab tanpa melempar balik pertanyaan ("How about you? / What about you?").

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **How would you describe today's weather to start a conversation in English?** / *Bagaimana kamu mendeskripsikan cuaca hari ini untuk memulai percakapan dalam bahasa Inggris?*
2. **Someone says "How are you?" to you. Give a natural, friendly reply and ask them back.** / *Seseorang berkata "How are you?" kepadamu. Berikan jawaban yang natural dan ramah, lalu tanya balik.*
3. **You are waiting in a queue and want to make small talk with the person next to you. What do you say?** / *Kamu sedang antri dan ingin berbicara ringan dengan orang di sebelahmu. Apa yang kamu katakan?*

Contoh referensi: *"It's quite hot today, isn't it? I hope it cools down later."* | *"I'm doing well, thank you! How about you? Are you having a good day?"* | *"Excuse me, is this queue for the counter? Have you been waiting long?"*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I am fine." (terlalu formal, tidak ada follow-up) → **"I'm doing well, thanks! How about you?"**; "The weather is hot." (statement flat) → **"It's quite hot today, isn't it?"** (tag question untuk engage); "How are you?" lagi sebagai balasan → **"How about you? / What about you?"**
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "weather" /ˈweðə/ (bukan /wea-ther/, th = /ð/ bersuara); "queue" /kjuː/ (sama dengan huruf Q, bukan /kwe/); "actually" /ˈæktʃuəli/ (AK-choo-uh-lee).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah user "melempar balik" pertanyaan atau hanya menjawab. Small talk yang baik adalah dua arah — jika satu arah saja, ini yang perlu diperbaiki.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "Not bad at all!" lebih bervariasi dari "I'm fine"; "Can't complain!" — ekspresi informal yang very natural; "What a small world!" — idiom untuk kebetulan bertemu kenalan.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: belajar tag questions — "Nice weather, isn't it?" / "You're from Jakarta, aren't you?" — ini membuat small talk terasa lebih natural dan mengundang respons.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Can you make small talk about a hobby or interest?" (topik lebih spesifik). Macet: Pattern Drill → "[Statement]. [Tag question]?" — contoh: "It's busy here today, isn't it?" — drill 3 situasi berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) melempar balik pertanyaan — "How about you?" setelah menjawab; (2) "active listening cues" — "Oh really? / That's nice!". Kalau user jawab sangat singkat, encourage: "Tell me a bit more — what else do you usually say?"

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab "I am fine. The weather is hot." → Tabel: flat, tidak ada follow-up → "I'm doing great, thanks! How about you? Pretty hot today, right?" (add tag question + follow-up). Pelafalan: "fine" /faɪn/ ok. Kelancaran: terlalu singkat dan kaku. Saran: "Can't complain!" sebagai variasi opener yang casual. CEFR: tambahkan tag question untuk naik ke A2.

### DAY 11 — Talking About Past Experiences

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menceritakan pengalaman yang sudah terjadi menggunakan Simple Past Tense. CEFR A1: *"can describe past activities and personal experiences using simple phrases."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) Simple Past regular: "I visited", "I watched", "I enjoyed"; (2) Simple Past irregular: "I went", "I ate", "I saw", "I had", "I made"; (3) waktu lampau: "last week", "yesterday", "two days ago", "last year", "when I was young"; (4) urutan cerita: "First...", "Then...", "After that...", "Finally...".
Fokus A1: bentuk past tense yang paling sering salah — "I go yesterday" → "I went yesterday"; "I eat sate" → "I ate sate". Untuk A1, fokus pada kata kerja yang paling umum (went, ate, saw, had, did, was/were).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Tell me about something interesting you did last week or last month.** / *Ceritakan sesuatu yang menarik yang kamu lakukan minggu lalu atau bulan lalu.*
2. **Describe a trip or holiday you took in the past. Where did you go? What did you do?** / *Deskripsikan perjalanan atau liburan yang pernah kamu lakukan. Ke mana kamu pergi? Apa yang kamu lakukan?*
3. **Tell me about a memorable meal or food experience you had in the past.** / *Ceritakan tentang pengalaman makan yang berkesan yang pernah kamu alami.*

Contoh referensi: *"Last week I went to a new restaurant with my family. We ate grilled fish and it was delicious."* | *"Two years ago I visited Bali. I went to the beach every day and tried many local foods."* | *"When I was a child, my grandmother made special fried rice. It was the best food I ever tasted."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I go last week" → **"I went last week"**; "I am eat nasi goreng" → **"I ate nasi goreng"**; "It was very enjoy" → **"It was very enjoyable"** (adjective, bukan verb).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "visited" /ˈvɪzɪtɪd/ (3 suku kata: VI-zi-tid); "experience" /ɪkˈspɪəriəns/ (ik-SPEER-ee-unce); "delicious" /dɪˈlɪʃəs/ (di-LISH-us, stress di tengah).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah ada sequence (first/then/after that). Narasi tanpa sequence terasa seperti daftar fakta, bukan cerita.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "It was absolutely amazing!" lebih ekspresif dari "It was good"; "I couldn't get enough of it" (tidak bisa berhenti makan/menikmati); "It brought back memories" untuk pengalaman nostalgia.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan detail deskriptif — bukan hanya APA yang terjadi, tapi BAGAIMANA rasanya — "The beach was so peaceful and the water was crystal clear."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Can you tell me about an experience you didn't enjoy? What went wrong?" (negative experience). Macet: Pattern Drill → "Last [time], I [went/ate/saw] [place/thing]. It was [adjective]." — drill 3 pengalaman.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) bentuk past tense (went/ate/saw/had) — paling kritis; (2) connector waktu (last week/yesterday/two years ago). Kalau user kesulitan dengan irregular verb, beri tabel kecil verba yang paling sering dipakai.

### DAY 12 — Talking About Future Plans

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Membicarakan rencana dan harapan di masa depan menggunakan "be going to" dan "will". CEFR A1: *"can make and respond to suggestions about what to do next."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) "be going to" untuk rencana yang sudah diputuskan — "I'm going to visit my family this weekend."; (2) "will" untuk keputusan spontan/prediksi — "It will be fun!", "I think I will try."; (3) "want to" untuk keinginan — "I want to learn guitar."; (4) waktu depan — "this weekend", "next month", "next year", "someday", "in the future".
Fokus A1: perbedaan "be going to" (sudah ada rencana) vs "will" (spontan/prediksi) — untuk A1, fokuskan pada "be going to" karena lebih predictable; "want to" untuk harapan umum. Error umum: "I will going to" (double future) → pilih salah satu saja.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **What are your plans for this weekend? Tell me what you are going to do.** / *Apa rencananmu untuk akhir pekan ini? Ceritakan apa yang akan kamu lakukan.*
2. **What do you want to do or achieve in the next one or two years?** / *Apa yang ingin kamu lakukan atau capai dalam satu atau dua tahun ke depan?*
3. **Describe your dream holiday — where would you go and what would you do there?** / *Deskripsikan liburan impianmu — ke mana kamu akan pergi dan apa yang akan kamu lakukan di sana?*

Contoh referensi: *"This weekend I'm going to clean my house and cook a nice meal. I think I'll also watch a movie."* | *"In the next year, I want to improve my English and get a promotion at work."* | *"My dream holiday is in Japan. I would visit Tokyo and try authentic ramen. I think it will be amazing."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I will going to" → **"I'm going to"** (hapus will); "I want go" → **"I want to go"** (to + infinitive wajib); "I go next week" → **"I'm going to go next week"** (future marker diperlukan).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "weekend" /ˌwiːkˈend/ (stress di akhir week-END); "achieve" /əˈtʃiːv/ (a-CHEEV, ch = /tʃ/); "future" /ˈfjuːtʃə/ (FYOO-cha, bukan /fu-tur/).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah user menyebut waktu yang spesifik (this weekend/next year) dan aktivitas yang konkret, bukan hanya "I want to be happy".
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I'm looking forward to [verb-ing]" sangat natural untuk rencana yang ditunggu; "I hope to [verb]" untuk harapan; "bucket list" untuk impian jangka panjang.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan alasan di balik rencana — "I'm going to study harder because I want to pass my English exam."

Prioritas: (1) "be going to" vs "will" — untuk A1 cukup "be going to" dulu; (2) "want to + infinitive" untuk harapan. Encourage user untuk menyebut detail spesifik — kapan dan apa tepatnya.

### DAY 14 — Talking About Frequency

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyatakan seberapa sering melakukan sesuatu menggunakan adverb dan expression of frequency. CEFR A1: *"can use simple expressions to talk about how often they do things."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) adverb of frequency: "always", "usually", "often", "sometimes", "rarely", "never" — posisi: sebelum verb utama, setelah be; (2) expression: "every day/week/month", "once/twice a week", "three times a month", "on Mondays"; (3) pertanyaan — "How often do you [verb]?", "Do you [verb] regularly?".
Fokus A1: posisi adverb of frequency — error umum "I go always to gym" → "I always go to the gym"; "She is never late" (setelah be) vs "She never comes late" (sebelum verb). Juga: "once" = 1 kali, "twice" = 2 kali, "three times" dst.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **How often do you exercise or do any physical activity? Tell me about your routine.** / *Seberapa sering kamu berolahraga atau melakukan aktivitas fisik? Ceritakan rutinitasmu.*
2. **How often do you speak English in your daily life? In what situations?** / *Seberapa sering kamu berbicara bahasa Inggris dalam kehidupan sehari-hari? Dalam situasi apa?*
3. **What do you do every single day without fail? Describe your most consistent habit.** / *Apa yang selalu kamu lakukan setiap hari tanpa gagal? Deskripsikan kebiasaanmu yang paling konsisten.*

Contoh referensi: *"I usually go for a walk three times a week. I rarely go to the gym because it is expensive."* | *"I sometimes speak English at work, especially in meetings. I never use English at home."* | *"I always drink coffee in the morning. I never start my day without it."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I go always" → **"I always go"** (adverb sebelum verb); "I am never late always" → **"I am never late"** (hanya satu adverb); "I do exercise every days" → **"I exercise every day"** (every day = singular, tanpa s).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "usually" /ˈjuːʒuəli/ (reinforcement dari Day 3); "rarely" /ˈreəli/ (RAIR-lee); "exercise" /ˈeksəsaɪz/ (EK-suh-size, 3 suku kata).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah posisi adverb konsisten dan tepat. Ini adalah pola yang perlu diulang banyak kali untuk jadi otomatis.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I make it a point to [verb]" = selalu menyempatkan; "without fail" = tanpa terkecuali (pola yang sudah ada di Q3); "I tend to [verb]" = kecenderungan umum, lebih nuanced dari "usually".
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: gabungkan frekuensi + kondisi — "I usually exercise in the morning, but when it rains, I skip it."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Has your routine changed recently? What did you do differently?" (past + present compare). Macet: Pattern Drill → "I [adverb] [verb] [expression of frequency]." — drill 5 kalimat berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) posisi adverb of frequency (before main verb, after be); (2) "once/twice/X times a week" — ekspresi yang sangat natural dan sering dipakai. Kalau user ragu, beri tabel sederhana: always (100%) → usually (80%) → often (60%) → sometimes (40%) → rarely (20%) → never (0%).

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)

I am drink coffee every morning always." → Tabel: "go always gym" → "always go to the gym"; "two times in week" → "twice a week"; "am drink coffee every morning always" → "always drink coffee every morning" (hapus am, hapus always di akhir). Pelafalan: "gym" /dʒɪm/ ok. Kelancaran: ada informasi konkret, structure perlu dibenahi. CEFR tip: gunakan "tend to" untuk variasi.

### DAY 15 — Giving Opinions

**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyampaikan pendapat dan pandangan dengan jelas dan sopan. CEFR A1: *"can express simple opinions using basic phrases, agreeing or disagreeing in a straightforward way."*

### DAY 16 — Agreeing and Disagreeing

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyatakan persetujuan dan ketidaksetujuan dengan sopan dan jelas. CEFR A1: *"can agree or disagree with a statement using simple phrases."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) setuju — "I agree.", "You're right.", "Exactly!", "That's true.", "I think so too."; (2) tidak setuju — "I disagree.", "I'm not sure about that.", "I don't think so.", "Actually, I think..."; (3) setuju sebagian — "I agree, but...", "You have a point, however...", "That's partly true, but..."; (4) memperhalus ketidaksetujuan — "With all due respect...", "I see your point, but...".
Fokus A1: cara tidak setuju yang SOPAN — di budaya Inggris/internasional, langsung "No, you're wrong!" terasa sangat blunt dan tidak sopan. "I'm not sure about that" atau "I see your point, but..." jauh lebih natural. Error umum juga: "I am agree" → "I agree" (agree adalah verb, bukan adjective).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Your friend says: "English is the most important language to learn." Do you agree or disagree? Why?** / *Temanmu berkata: "Bahasa Inggris adalah bahasa terpenting untuk dipelajari." Apakah kamu setuju atau tidak? Kenapa?*
2. **Someone says: "Working from home is better than working at the office." What do you think?** / *Seseorang berkata: "Bekerja dari rumah lebih baik daripada di kantor." Apa pendapatmu?*
3. **Do you agree that exercise is more important than diet for good health? Share your view.** / *Apakah kamu setuju bahwa olahraga lebih penting daripada diet untuk kesehatan yang baik? Bagikan pandanganmu.*

Contoh referensi: *"I agree that English is very important. You're right — it opens many doors."* | *"I'm not sure about that. I think working at the office helps with teamwork. But I see your point about flexibility."* | *"I agree, but I think both are equally important. Exercise and diet work together."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I am agree" → **"I agree"** (verb langsung); "I am not agree" → **"I disagree"** atau **"I don't agree"**; "You are right about" → **"You're right about"** (kontraksi lebih natural dalam speaking).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "disagree" /ˌdɪsəˈɡriː/ (dis-uh-GREE, stress di akhir); "exactly" /ɪɡˈzæktli/ (ig-ZAKT-lee); "point" /pɔɪnt/ (sering salah jadi /po-int/ — harus satu suku kata).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah user hanya bilang "I agree" tanpa alasan. Agreeing/disagreeing yang efektif selalu disertai alasan singkat.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "Fair point!" — mengakui argumen lawan dengan elegan; "I couldn't agree more!" — persetujuan yang sangat kuat; "That's debatable." — menunjukkan bahwa topik masih bisa diperdebatkan.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: belajar partial agreement — "I agree to some extent, but..." + alasan yang lebih detail dan balanced.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Can you think of one more reason to support your position?" (deepen argument). Macet: Pattern Drill → "I agree/disagree because [reason]. I think [your view]." — drill 3 statement berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) "I agree" bukan "I am agree"; (2) cara tidak setuju yang sopan — "I'm not sure about that" atau "I see your point, but...". Kalau user terasa ragu-ragu, validasi: "Tidak ada jawaban yang salah — yang penting komunikasikan pendapatmu dengan jelas!"

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)

Pelafalan: "important" /ɪmˈpɔːtənt/. Kelancaran: ada persetujuan + alasan, struktur perlu perbaikan. Saran: "Absolutely! English opens so many doors." lebih expressive.

### DAY 17 — Making Suggestions

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Membuat dan merespons saran dengan sopan dalam berbagai situasi. CEFR A1: *"can make and respond to simple suggestions."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) membuat saran — "Why don't you [verb]?", "You should [verb].", "How about [verb-ing]?", "Maybe you could [verb].", "Have you tried [verb-ing]?"; (2) menerima saran — "That's a great idea!", "Good idea!", "I'll try that."; (3) menolak saran dengan sopan — "Thanks for the suggestion, but...", "I've already tried that.", "I'm not sure that will work.".
Fokus A1: "should" adalah modal paling umum untuk saran — "You should rest more." Error umum: "You must rest" (terlalu kuat/memaksa) — "should" lebih appropriate untuk saran; "Why don't you to go" → "Why don't you go" (tidak perlu "to" setelah don't you).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Your friend says they feel stressed and very tired lately. What do you suggest they do?** / *Temanmu bilang mereka merasa stres dan sangat lelah belakangan ini. Apa yang kamu sarankan?*
2. **A tourist asks you for suggestions on what to do and see in your city. What do you recommend?** / *Seorang turis meminta saranmu tentang apa yang bisa dilakukan dan dilihat di kotamu. Apa yang kamu rekomendasikan?*
3. **A friend wants to improve their English quickly. What suggestions do you have for them?** / *Seorang teman ingin meningkatkan bahasa Inggrisnya dengan cepat. Apa saran-saranmu untuk mereka?*

Contoh referensi: *"You should take a break and sleep early. Why don't you try meditation? It really helps with stress."* | *"I suggest visiting the old town area. How about trying the local street food? It's a must!"* | *"You should practice every day, even for fifteen minutes. Why don't you watch English movies with subtitles?"*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "Why don't you to rest" → **"Why don't you rest"** (hapus "to"); "You must go" (terlalu strong) → **"You should go"** atau **"I suggest going"**; "Maybe you could to try" → **"Maybe you could try"** (hapus "to" setelah modal).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "suggest" /səˈdʒest/ (suh-JEST, g tidak berbunyi terpisah); "recommend" /ˌrekəˈmend/ (rek-uh-MEND, stress di akhir); "meditation" /ˌmedɪˈteɪʃən/ (med-ih-TAY-shun).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah ada lebih dari satu saran dengan variasi ekspresi (should/why don't/how about). Satu ekspresi yang diulang terasa monoton.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "You might want to [verb]" lebih halus dari "you should"; "It might help to..." sangat natural untuk konteks saran; "Give it a shot!" — ekspresi casual untuk mendorong mencoba sesuatu.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan alasan di balik saran — "You should sleep early because lack of sleep makes stress worse."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "What would you suggest if someone has very limited time and money?" (constraint-based suggestion). Macet: Pattern Drill → "You should [verb]. Why don't you [verb]? How about [verb-ing]?" — drill dengan topik yang sama.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) "should" untuk saran yang paling umum; (2) variasi ekspresi — jangan hanya "you should" tiga kali berturut-turut. Encourage user untuk memberikan minimal 2 saran berbeda dengan ekspresi berbeda.

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab "You must sleep early and you must do yoga." → Tabel: "you must sleep" → "you should sleep" (saran, bukan kewajiban); "you must do yoga" → "why don't you try yoga?" (lebih inviting). Pelafalan: "yoga" /ˈjəʊɡə/ ok. Kelancaran: ada 2 saran tapi ekspresi monoton. Saran: variasikan dengan "How about trying yoga?" Upgrade: tambahkan alasan.

### DAY 18 — Making Invitations

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Mengundang orang lain ke suatu acara atau kegiatan, serta menerima atau menolak undangan dengan sopan. CEFR A1: *"can make and respond to simple invitations."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) mengundang — "Would you like to [verb]?", "Do you want to [verb]?", "Are you free [time]?", "How about joining me for [activity]?"; (2) menerima undangan — "I'd love to!", "That sounds great!", "Sure, why not?", "Count me in!"; (3) menolak dengan sopan — "I'm sorry, I can't.", "Thanks for the invite, but...", "I wish I could, but I have [reason].", "Maybe next time?".
Fokus A1: perbedaan "I'd love to" (menerima) vs "I'd love to, but..." (menolak dengan sopan) — penambahan "but" mengubah makna sepenuhnya. Error umum: "I cannot go." (terlalu blunt) → "I'm sorry, I can't make it. Maybe next time?" (lebih natural dan sopan).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **You want to invite a friend to try a new restaurant you discovered. How do you invite them?** / *Kamu ingin mengajak teman mencoba restoran baru yang kamu temukan. Bagaimana kamu mengundang mereka?*
2. **You want to invite a colleague to join a team lunch tomorrow. What do you say to them?** / *Kamu ingin mengajak rekan kerja untuk ikut makan siang bersama tim besok. Apa yang kamu katakan?*
3. **A friend invites you to their birthday party but you already have other plans. How do you decline politely?** / *Seorang teman mengundangmu ke pesta ulang tahun mereka tapi kamu sudah punya rencana lain. Bagaimana kamu menolak dengan sopan?*

Contoh referensi: *"Hey, would you like to try this new Japanese restaurant? I heard the food is amazing!"* | *"Are you free tomorrow at noon? We're all going for team lunch — you should join us!"* | *"Thank you so much for the invite! I'm so sorry, I can't make it — I have a prior commitment. I hope you have a wonderful time!"*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I cannot go" → **"I'm sorry, I can't make it"** (lebih natural dan sopan); "You want come?" → **"Would you like to come?"** (formal invitation form); "I'd love to but I busy" → **"I'd love to, but I'm busy"** (be verb diperlukan).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "invitation" /ˌɪnvɪˈteɪʃən/ (in-vi-TAY-shun); "commit" /kəˈmɪt/ (kuh-MIT); "birthday" /ˈbɜːθdeɪ/ (BURTH-day, th = /θ/ tak bersuara).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah undangan terdengar genuine dan warm, atau terlalu kaku/formal. "Hey, would you like to...?" terasa lebih friendly dari "Do you want to come?" untuk konteks informal.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I wouldn't miss it for the world!" (penerimaan yang sangat enthusiastic); "Rain check?" (ajak ulang di waktu lain); "I'll have to take a rain check on that." (idiom penolakan yang sopan dan natural).
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: belajar mengundang dengan conditional — "If you're free this Saturday, would you like to join us for a picnic?"
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "How would you invite someone to a more formal event, like a work presentation?" (register shift). Macet: Pattern Drill → "Would you like to [activity]? / I'm sorry, I can't [reason]. Maybe [alternative]?" — drill 3 skenario.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara

### DAY 20 — Offering Help

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menawarkan bantuan dan menerima/menolak tawaran bantuan dengan sopan. CEFR A1: *"can offer help and respond to offers of help using simple phrases."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) menawarkan — "Can I help you?", "Would you like some help?", "Let me help you with that.", "Do you need a hand?", "Is there anything I can do?"; (2) menerima tawaran — "Yes, please! That would be great.", "Thank you, I'd appreciate that.", "That's very kind of you!"; (3) menolak tawaran — "No, thank you. I'm fine.", "I'm okay, but thank you!", "I think I can manage, thanks.".
Fokus A1: "Do you need a hand?" adalah idiom yang sangat natural (bukan makna literal tangan). Error umum: "Can I help to you?" → "Can I help you?" (tanpa "to"); "I want help you" → "Let me help you" atau "I'd like to help you".

4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "Allow me" (sangat polite, formal) untuk situasi resmi; "Happy to help!" setelah menerima ucapan terima kasih; "No trouble at all!" untuk menunjukkan bahwa membantu bukan masalah.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: belajar menawarkan bantuan dengan conditional — "If you need any help, feel free to let me know." atau "Just say the word and I'll be there!"
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "What would you do if someone refused your help but clearly still needed it?" (persistence). Macet: Pattern Drill → "Do you need help with [specific task]? Let me [verb] for you." — drill 3 situasi berbeda.

### DAY 21 — Talking About Problems and Solutions

### DAY 22 — Explaining Something Clearly

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menjelaskan proses, prosedur, atau konsep dengan jelas dan runtut. CEFR A1: *"can describe everyday activities using simple, sequential language."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) sequence connector — "First...", "Then...", "Next...", "After that...", "Finally..."; (2) instruksi — "You need to [verb].", "You should [verb].", "Make sure you [verb]."; (3) klarifikasi — "What I mean is...", "In other words...", "Basically..."; (4) cek pemahaman — "Does that make sense?", "Is that clear?".
Fokus A1: pentingnya sequence connectors — tanpa them, penjelasan terasa seperti daftar random. Error umum: "You put sugar then add water then boil" (terlalu bersambung tanpa connector yang jelas) → "First, you add sugar. Then, you pour in water. Finally, you boil it." Gunakan kalimat pendek yang terpisah, bukan satu kalimat panjang.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Explain how you make your favorite simple dish or drink. Walk me through the steps.** / *Jelaskan cara membuat makanan atau minuman sederhana favoritmu. Ceritakan langkah-langkahnya.*
2. **Explain what your job or daily work involves to someone who knows nothing about it.** / *Jelaskan apa yang kamu kerjakan sehari-hari kepada seseorang yang tidak tahu sama sekali tentang pekerjaanmu.*
3. **Explain how to use a simple but useful app or feature on your phone.** / *Jelaskan cara menggunakan aplikasi atau fitur sederhana tapi berguna di ponselmu.*

Contoh referensi: *"To make instant noodles, first you boil water. Then, you put the noodles in for three minutes. After that, you add the seasoning. Finally, it is ready to eat."* | *"I work as a teacher. Basically, I explain lessons, give exercises, and check student work. The most important part is making sure students understand."* | *"WhatsApp is easy. First, you open the app. Then, you tap the chat icon. Next, you search for a contact. Finally, you type your message and send."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: tidak ada sequence connector → tambahkan first/then/after that/finally; "you boil until boil" → **"you boil until it boils"** (subject "it" diperlukan); "it is simple to make" di awal (terlalu general) → mulai langsung dengan langkah pertama.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "basically" /ˈbeɪsɪkli/ (BAY-sik-lee, 3 suku kata); "finally" /ˈfaɪnəli/ (FY-nuh-lee); "feature" /ˈfiːtʃə/ (FEE-cha, bukan "fe-a-ture").
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah ada urutan yang logis dan lengkap. Penjelasan yang bagus = bisa diikuti orang yang belum pernah melakukannya.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "step by step" untuk menekankan proses; "The key is to [verb]" untuk menyoroti langkah terpenting; "It's quite straightforward" untuk mendeskripsikan proses yang mudah.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan detail kondisi — "Make sure the water is boiling before you add the noodles, otherwise they won't cook properly."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Can you explain a more complex process — like how you plan your week or manage your money?" (abstract explanation). Macet: Pattern Drill → "First, [step 1]. Then, [step 2]. Finally, [last step]." — drill dengan 3 proses berbeda.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) sequence connectors (first/then/finally) — wajib ada; (2) kalimat pendek yang jelas, bukan satu kalimat panjang bersambung. Kalau user menjelaskan terlalu cepat tanpa struktur, tanya: "Can you tell me each step one by one?"

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab "To make coffee you put coffee then water then sugar then stir." → Tabel: tidak ada connector → "First, you put coffee in the cup. Then, you add hot water. After that, you add sugar. Finally, you stir until it dissolves." Pelafalan: "dissolve" /dɪˈzɒlv/. Kelancaran: informasi ada tapi terasa rush. Saran: "The key is to stir well" untuk closure yang natural. CEFR: tambahkan kondisi.

### DAY 23 — Comparing People, Things, and Places

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Membandingkan dua hal — orang, benda, atau tempat — menggunakan comparative dan superlative. CEFR A1: *"can use simple comparatives and superlatives to describe and compare people and things."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) comparative — "[A] is [adjective]-er than [B]" atau "[A] is more [adjective] than [B]"; (2) superlative — "[A] is the [adjective]-est" atau "[A] is the most [adjective]"; (3) similarity — "[A] and [B] are both...", "[A] is similar to [B]"; (4) perbedaan — "The main difference is...", "Unlike [A], [B] is...".
Fokus A1: aturan pembentukan comparative — kata pendek (1-2 suku kata) + er: bigger, cheaper, older. Kata panjang (3+ suku kata) + more: more expensive, more interesting. Irregular: good → better, bad → worse, far → farther. Error umum: "more bigger" → "bigger" (double comparative); "the most biggest" → "the biggest".

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Compare your city to the capital city of your country. What are the main differences?** / *Bandingkan kotamu dengan ibu kota negaramu. Apa perbedaan utamanya?*
2. **Compare two types of transport you use regularly. Which do you prefer and why?** / *Bandingkan dua jenis transportasi yang sering kamu gunakan. Mana yang lebih kamu sukai dan kenapa?*
3. **Compare yourself now to yourself five years ago. How have you changed?** / *Bandingkan dirimu sekarang dengan dirimu lima tahun lalu. Bagaimana kamu berubah?*

Contoh referensi: *"My city is smaller and quieter than Jakarta. Jakarta is more modern and more crowded. But I think my city is cleaner and more peaceful."* | *"I usually take the bus or ojek. The bus is cheaper but slower. Ojek is faster but more expensive. I prefer ojek because it saves time."* | *"Five years ago I was less confident and more shy. Now I am more outgoing and I speak more. I think I have become more mature."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "more bigger" → **"bigger"** (hapus more); "Jakarta is more busier" → **"Jakarta is busier"**; "I was more shy before" → **"I was shyer before"** atau "I was more shy" (kedua-duanya ok untuk shy); "He is more good" → **"He is better"** (irregular).
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "comparison" /kəmˈpærɪsən/ (kum-PAIR-ih-sun); "crowded" /ˈkraʊdɪd/ (CROWD-id); "mature" /məˈtjʊə/ (muh-TYOOR di UK English).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah ada struktur "A dibanding B" yang jelas, bukan hanya mendeskripsikan A tanpa menyebut B. Komparasi yang baik = dua entitas yang jelas dibandingkan.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "worlds apart" (sangat berbeda sekali); "neck and neck" (sangat mirip/setara); "hands down" (tanpa keraguan) — "Ojek is hands down faster" — idiom yang sangat natural.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — naik ke A2: tambahkan contoh konkret — "Jakarta is more expensive. For example, rent costs three times more than in my city."
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: "Can you compare two famous Indonesian dishes — which is better for a foreigner to try first?" (recommendation context). Macet: Pattern Drill → "[A] is [comparative adjective] than [B]. I prefer [A/B] because [reason]." — drill 4 pasang perbandingan.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara
Prioritas: (1) comparative form yang benar (shorter adjectives + er, longer + more); (2) irregular forms (good→better, bad→worse). Kalau user ragu, beri contoh quick: "big → bigger, expensive → more expensive, good → better."

#### 🗣️ Detail Practice (Speaking Mode)
Skenario: user jawab "My city is more smaller than Jakarta. Jakarta is more crowded and more bigger." → Tabel: "more smaller" → "smaller" (hapus more); "more bigger" → "bigger" (hapus more, double error). Pelafalan: "crowded" /ˈkraʊdɪd/. Kelancaran: ada ide perbandingan yang baik. Saran: "Jakarta is hands down bigger" untuk idiom yang natural. CEFR: tambahkan contoh konkret.

### DAY 24 — Telling a Short Story

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menceritakan kisah pendek yang menarik dengan struktur yang jelas — awal, tengah, dan akhir. CEFR A1: *"can tell a simple story about a personal experience using basic narrative structure."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) pembuka cerita — "This happened last [time]...", "Let me tell you about...", "One day...", "I remember when..."; (2) sequence — "First...", "Then...", "Suddenly...", "After that...", "In the end..."; (3) reaksi emosi — "I was shocked!", "It was hilarious!", "I couldn't believe it!", "I felt so embarrassed."; (4) penutup — "Looking back, it was [adjective].", "It turned out to be...".
Fokus A1: "Suddenly" adalah kata kunci yang membuat cerita terasa hidup dan narrative. Error umum: cerita tanpa emosi/reaksi → tambahkan setidaknya satu ekspresi perasaan. Juga: semua dalam Simple Past — "I went", "I saw", "I felt".

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Tell me a short funny or embarrassing story that happened to you recently.** / *Ceritakan sebuah kisah lucu atau memalukan yang terjadi padamu belakangan ini.*
2. **Tell me about a time when something went wrong but turned out okay in the end.** / *Ceritakan tentang suatu saat ketika sesuatu berjalan tidak sesuai rencana tapi akhirnya baik-baik saja.*
3. **Tell me a story about a memorable or special moment with your family or friends.** / *Ceritakan sebuah momen berkesan atau spesial yang kamu alami bersama keluarga atau teman.*

Contoh referensi: *"Last week something funny happened. I was on my way to work and I wore two different shoes! I didn't notice until I got to the office. Everyone laughed and I felt so embarrassed!"* | *"One time I missed my bus to work. I thought I would be very late. But then my neighbor offered me a lift and I arrived on time. It turned out okay!"* | *"I remember when my whole family went to the beach last year. We laughed, played, and ate together. It was one of the best days of the year."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I don't notice until" → **"I didn't notice until"** (past tense wajib di semua bagian cerita); "It is funny" (present) → **"It was funny"** (past, cerita sudah terjadi); "The end it is okay" → **"In the end, it was okay"**.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "embarrassed" /ɪmˈbærəst/ (im-BA-rust, 3 suku kata, stress di tengah); "memorable" /ˈmemərəbəl/ (MEM-uh-ruh-bul); "hilarious" /hɪˈleəriəs/ (hi-LAIR-ee-us).

### DAY 25 — Giving Instructions

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Memberikan instruksi yang jelas, tepat, dan mudah diikuti — untuk memasak, menuju ke tempat tertentu, atau menggunakan perangkat. CEFR A1: *"can give simple step-by-step instructions using imperative forms and sequence words."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) imperative — "Press [button].", "Turn [knob/handle].", "Add [ingredient].", "Wait for [X] minutes."; (2) connector — "First...", "Then...", "Next...", "After that...", "Finally..."; (3) warning/catatan penting — "Be careful not to [verb].", "Make sure [clause].", "Don't forget to [verb]."; (4) estimasi — "It takes about [X] minutes.", "Cook for [X] minutes on [heat level] heat.".
Fokus A1: imperative adalah bentuk instruksi paling natural — "Add sugar" bukan "You should add sugar" (meskipun benar, imperative lebih direct dan tepat untuk instruksi). Error umum: "You are pressing the button" (progressive) → "Press the button" (imperative).

### DAY 26 — Making Complaints Politely

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyampaikan keluhan dengan sopan dan efektif — di restoran, hotel, atau layanan pelanggan. CEFR A1: *"can make a simple complaint and explain what is wrong."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) pembuka keluhan yang sopan — "Excuse me, I'm afraid there's a problem.", "I'm sorry to bother you, but...", "I'd like to report an issue."; (2) menjelaskan masalah — "This is not what I ordered.", "There seems to be a mistake.", "The [item] is [adjective] and I'm not happy with it."; (3) meminta solusi — "Could you please [verb]?", "I'd like a replacement/refund.", "Is there anything you can do?"; (4) mengakhiri dengan baik — "Thank you for your help.", "I appreciate it.".
Fokus A1: keluhan yang dimulai dengan "Excuse me" dan "I'm sorry to bother you" jauh lebih effective daripada langsung menyerang. Error umum: "This food is terrible!" (terlalu aggressive) → "Excuse me, I think this might not be what I ordered — could you check for me?" (polite + specific).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **You ordered pasta at a restaurant but received noodle soup instead. How do you complain politely to the waiter?** / *Kamu memesan pasta di restoran tapi malah mendapat sup mie. Bagaimana kamu mengeluh dengan sopan kepada pelayan?*
2. **Your hotel room is too noisy because of construction nearby and you can't sleep. How do you complain to the receptionist?** / *Kamar hotelmu sangat berisik karena ada konstruksi di dekatnya dan kamu tidak bisa tidur. Bagaimana kamu mengeluh kepada resepsionis?*
3. **A product you bought online arrived damaged. How do you contact customer service to complain?** / *Produk yang kamu beli online tiba dalam keadaan rusak. Bagaimana kamu menghubungi layanan pelanggan untuk mengeluh?*

The item arrived damaged. I'd like to request a replacement or a refund, please."*

2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "complaint" /kəmˈpleɪnt/ (kum-PLAYNT, stress di akhir); "replacement" /rɪˈpleɪsmənt/ (ri-PLACE-munt); "appreciate" /əˈpriːʃieɪt/ (a-PREE-shee-ayt).

4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I'm afraid there's an issue" lebih sophisticated dari "There is problem"; "bear with me" untuk meminta kesabaran; "I'd appreciate it if you could [verb]" sangat polite dan effective.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.

### DAY 27 — Talking About Expectations

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyampaikan harapan, ekspektasi, dan membandingkan apa yang diharapkan vs kenyataan. CEFR A1: *"can describe what they expected and what actually happened, using simple contrast phrases."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) ekspektasi — "I expected...", "I thought...", "I was hoping...", "I assumed..."; (2) kontras — "But actually...", "In reality...", "It turned out that...", "Surprisingly..."; (3) harapan masa depan — "I hope that [clause].", "I expect [noun/clause].", "I'm looking forward to [noun/verb-ing]."; (4) ekspektasi vs kenyataan — "It was better/worse than I expected.", "It was not what I imagined."
Fokus A1: "I expected" (past) untuk ekspektasi yang sudah terjadi vs "I expect/hope" (present) untuk harapan masa depan. Error umum: "I expect it was good" → "I expected it to be good" (past + to-infinitive untuk reported expectation) atau "I thought it would be good" (past conditional).

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)

**Fungsi komunikatif:** Membandingkan kebiasaan lama dan sekarang menggunakan "used to" dan present simple. CEFR A1: *"can describe past routines and compare them with present habits using simple language."*

### DAY 29 — Talking About Preferences

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyampaikan preferensi dan pilihan pribadi dengan alasan yang jelas. CEFR A1: *"can express preferences on everyday topics using basic comparative structures."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) "I prefer [A] to [B]" atau "I prefer [verb-ing] to [verb-ing]"; (2) "I would rather [verb] than [verb]" — lebih memilih melakukan A daripada B; (3) "[A] suits me better because..."; (4) "Given a choice, I would choose [A]."; (5) "I'm more of a [A] person" — ekspresi identifikasi preferensi yang sangat natural.
Fokus A1: "I prefer A to B" bukan "I prefer A than B" (perlu "to", bukan "than") — ini adalah error paling umum. Juga: "I would rather go than stay" (infinitive tanpa to setelah "rather than") — "I would rather go than to stay" salah.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Do you prefer mornings or evenings? Tell me which you prefer and why.** / *Apakah kamu lebih suka pagi hari atau malam hari? Ceritakan mana yang lebih kamu sukai dan kenapa.*
2. **Do you prefer working alone or working with a team? Explain your preference.** / *Apakah kamu lebih suka bekerja sendiri atau bersama tim? Jelaskan preferensimu.*
3. **Would you prefer to live in a big city or a small town? Give your reasons.** / *Apakah kamu lebih suka tinggal di kota besar atau kota kecil? Berikan alasanmu.*

Contoh referensi: *"I prefer evenings to mornings. I feel more creative and relaxed at night. I'm definitely more of an evening person."* | *"I prefer working in a team. I think collaboration produces better ideas. But sometimes I need quiet time to focus alone."* | *"I would rather live in a small town than a big city. It is quieter and less stressful. However, I do enjoy visiting big cities for the culture and food."*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — error umum: "I prefer morning than evening" → **"I prefer mornings to evenings"** (to, bukan than); "I would rather to go" → **"I would rather go"** (hapus "to" setelah rather); "I more like team work" → **"I prefer working in a team"** atau **"I like teamwork more"**.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "prefer" /prɪˈfɜː/ (pri-FER, stress di akhir); "collaboration" /kəˌlæbəˈreɪʃən/ (kuh-lab-uh-RAY-shun); "creative" /kriˈeɪtɪv/ (kree-AY-tiv).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — cek apakah preferensi disertai alasan ("because..."). Preferensi tanpa alasan terasa seperti pilihan yang tidak dipikirkan — less convincing.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I'm more of a [noun] person" sangat natural — "I'm more of a night owl", "I'm more of a team player"; "suits me better" = lebih cocok untukku; "I can't imagine living without [thing]" untuk preferensi yang sangat kuat.
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.

#### 💬 Detail Panduan Kak Ara

Pelafalan: "prefer" /prɪˈfɜː/. Kelancaran: ada alasan singkat, grammar perlu konsistensi. Saran: "I'm more of a morning person" untuk ekspresi yang lebih natural. CEFR: tambahkan nuanced view.

#### 📚 Detail Materi
**Fungsi komunikatif:** Menyampaikan presentasi mini yang terstruktur dan confident — pembuka, isi, dan penutup. CEFR A1: *"can give a very short, rehearsed presentation on a familiar topic, using simple language."*
Ekspresi kunci: (1) pembuka — "Hello everyone, my name is [name].", "Today I'd like to talk about...", "I'm going to tell you about..."; (2) isi — sequence words (first/second/finally) + poin utama dengan detail singkat; (3) transisi — "Moving on to...", "Another thing I want to mention is..."; (4) penutup — "To sum up...", "In conclusion...", "That's all for now. Thank you for listening!"; (5) tanya jawab — "Does anyone have any questions?"
Fokus A1: mini presentation harus terstruktur (ada awal-tengah-akhir) meskipun pendek. Error umum: langsung menyampaikan isi tanpa pembuka dan penutup — terasa incomplete. Juga: tidak perlu sempurna — "um" dan "uh" adalah bagian normal dari speaking, yang penting isi tersampaikan dengan jelas.

#### ✅ Detail Task (3 Speaking Questions)
1. **Give a short presentation (about 1-2 minutes) introducing yourself: your name, where you are from, your job or study, and one interesting fact about yourself.** / *Berikan presentasi singkat (sekitar 1-2 menit) memperkenalkan dirimu: namamu, asalmu, pekerjaanmu atau studimu, dan satu fakta menarik tentang dirimu.*
2. **Give a short presentation about your favorite topic, hobby, or interest. Tell us what it is, why you love it, and one thing others might not know about it.** / *Berikan presentasi singkat tentang topik, hobi, atau minatmu yang paling kamu sukai. Ceritakan apa itu, kenapa kamu menyukainya, dan satu hal yang mungkin orang lain tidak tahu tentangnya.*
3. **Give a short presentation about your English learning journey: where you started, what you have learned, and what you hope to achieve next.** / *Berikan presentasi singkat tentang perjalanan belajar bahasa Inggrismu: dari mana kamu mulai, apa yang sudah kamu pelajari, dan apa yang kamu harapkan akan kamu capai selanjutnya.*

Contoh referensi: *"Hello! My name is Budi and I'm from Bandung. I work as a software developer and I've been learning English for about six months. One interesting fact about me is that I can solve a Rubik's cube in under two minutes! That's a little bit about me. Thank you!"* | *"Today I'd like to talk about my favorite hobby: photography. I love capturing everyday moments and turning them into memories. What most people don't know is that you don't need an expensive camera — my best photos were taken with my phone! In conclusion, photography is something everyone can enjoy."* | *"When I started, I couldn't say even one sentence in English. Over the past thirty days, I've learned how to introduce myself, give directions, tell stories, and much more. My next goal is to have a real conversation with a native speaker. Thank you for joining my English journey!"*

#### 🔉 Detail Feedback 8 Langkah
1️⃣ **Tabel Perbandingan** — cek kelengkapan struktur: ada pembuka ("Hello, my name is...") + isi (minimal 3 poin) + penutup ("Thank you"). Komponen yang hilang = area yang perlu diperkuat.
2️⃣ **Catatan Pelafalan** — "conclusion" /kənˈkluːʒən/ (kun-KLOO-zhun); "presentation" /ˌprezənˈteɪʃən/ (prez-un-TAY-shun); "journey" /ˈdʒɜːni/ (JER-nee).
3️⃣ **Analisa Kelancaran** — ini adalah culmination dari 30 hari belajar — cek apakah user bisa berbicara selama 1 menit penuh dengan topik yang dipilih. Jika ya, ini adalah milestone yang luar biasa untuk A1.
4️⃣ **Discourse & Coherence Check** — respons user runtut sebagai giliran percakapan? Ada ide utama + pendukung? Jika ada gap koneksi antar kalimat: tunjuk 1 + saran perbaikan singkat. Sudah runtut: ✅.
5️⃣ **Saran Pilihan Kata** — "I'd like to leave you with a thought..." untuk ending yang memorable; "It's been quite a journey!" untuk refleksi; "Thank you for your time and attention" lebih formal dari sekadar "Thank you".
6️⃣ **Pragmatic Register Check** — register respons user sesuai konteks topik hari ini? Terlalu formal/kasual? Berikan 1 frasa alternatif yang lebih pas register-nya + alasan singkat. Sudah sesuai: ✅.
7️⃣ **CEFR tip** — selamat! Menyelesaikan 30 sesi speaking berarti sudah melampaui upper-A1 dalam speaking skill. Goals untuk A2: (1) lebih panjang dan detail; (2) gunakan conditional lebih sering; (3) presentasi tanpa notenotes sama sekali.
8️⃣ **Adaptasi** — lancar: minta user untuk improvise a Q&A session — "Imagine someone just asked you a question about your presentation. What did they ask and how would you answer?" Macet: bantu user dengan outline prompt → "Coba ikuti struktur ini: Hello, my name is [name]. Today I want to talk about [topic]. First, [point 1]. Second, [point 2]. In conclusion, [summary]. Thank you!"
